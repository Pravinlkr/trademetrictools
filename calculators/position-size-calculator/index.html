Why Position Sizing Is Critical in Trading

Many traders focus heavily on strategy but ignore risk management. Without proper position sizing, even profitable systems can fail due to oversized losses. Professional traders typically risk no more than 0.5% to 1% of their total capital per trade.

Limiting risk to 0.5–1% helps:

  • Protect trading capital during losing streaks
  • Reduce emotional decision making
  • Maintain long-term consistency
  • Prevent large drawdowns
  • Improve psychological discipline

How This Position Size Calculator Works

The calculator uses the standard professional formula:

Position Size = (Account Size × Risk %) ÷ (Entry − Stop Loss)

After calculating the number of shares, it automatically computes your target price using the selected risk-reward ratio. This means you instantly know:

  • Exact number of shares to buy
  • Total amount at risk
  • Target price based on R-multiple
  • Structured trade plan before entry

Example

If your account size is ₹10,000 and you risk 0.5% per trade, your maximum risk is ₹50. If you enter at ₹100 with a stop loss at ₹95, your risk per share is ₹5. The calculator will suggest buying 10 shares. If you choose a 1:5 risk-reward ratio, your target price will be ₹125.
